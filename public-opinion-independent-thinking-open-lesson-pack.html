Opening question

When most people approve of something, does that make it wiser, safer, or just louder?

Read several source fragments first. Then decide whether public opinion is a useful moral signal, a form of social pressure, or something in between.

Position duel

Public opinion can protect us.

It gives quick feedback about what a community finds fair, dangerous, shameful, or admirable.

Try saying: Public opinion is imperfect, but it can function as an early warning system.

Public opinion can trap us.

It rewards conformity and makes honest disagreement look eccentric, rude, or risky.

Try saying: The problem is not disagreement itself, but the social cost of being seen to disagree.

The mature answer is conditional.

We should listen to public opinion, but not let approval replace judgment.

Try saying: Public opinion deserves attention, not obedience.

Speaking task

Prompt: Some people say that young people should care less about what others think. Others argue that sensitivity to public opinion is part of becoming socially responsible. Which view is closer to yours?

  1. Open with a balanced sentence.
  2. Use one source fragment as your example.
  3. Give one personal or school-life situation.
  4. End with a conditional judgment: when public opinion helps, and when it harms.

90-second shape

0-20 sec: stance with concession.

20-55 sec: source-based example.

55-80 sec: student-life application.

80-90 sec: final criterion.

Writing prompt

In modern life, people often adjust their opinions, language, or behavior because of public opinion. Write an essay discussing whether this pressure makes people more responsible or less independent. Use at least one source fragment from this page and one example from school, media, or family life.

Upgrade checklist

  • Does your essay distinguish social pressure from moral responsibility?
  • Did you use a source fragment as evidence, not decoration?
  • Did you avoid a simple yes/no answer?
  • Did you include one sentence with although / while / in that / not because...but because?
