On the Decay of the Art of Lying

Mark Twain · ethical_contrast · core_debate · B2-C1

brutal_truthinjurytact

An injurious truth has no merit over an injurious lie.

Classroom angle

This is today’s strongest debate seed: truth is not automatically virtue; if truth causes unnecessary harm, it may lose moral superiority.

Discussion: Do you agree that a harmful truth is no better than a harmful lie?
Sentence model: Honesty loses its moral force when it becomes a way to hurt rather than to help.
Activity: Students create two examples: one necessary truth, one injurious truth.
