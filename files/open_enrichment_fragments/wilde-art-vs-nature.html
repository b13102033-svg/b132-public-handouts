Intentions: The Decay of Lying

Oscar Wilde · literature_aesthetics · creative_extension · C1

fiction art truth nature

What Art really reveals to us is Nature’s lack of design.

Classroom angle

Wilde places fiction and art above factual truth. Use this to ask whether novels, rhetoric, or white lies can create a more useful kind of truth.

Discussion: Can fiction reveal truth by refusing to be factual?
Sentence model: Fiction may be factually false but emotionally or morally revealing.
Activity: Students compare “factually true” and “emotionally true”.
