Pride and Prejudice

Jane Austen · novel_social_psychology · lexical_nuance · B2-C1

pride vanity self_image social_image

Pride relates more to our opinion of ourselves; vanity to what we would have others think of us.

Classroom angle

Use this card to connect white lies with self-image versus social image: people often lie not for truth, but for the image they want to maintain.

Discussion: Do people lie more to protect others, or to protect their own image?
Sentence model: Many social lies protect not the listener’s feelings but the speaker’s desired image.
Activity: Students classify a lie as pride-driven or vanity-driven.
