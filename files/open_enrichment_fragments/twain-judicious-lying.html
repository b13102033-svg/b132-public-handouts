On the Decay of the Art of Lying

Mark Twain · rhetoric_ethics · argument_strength · C1

judicious white_lie ethics

Judicious lying is what the world needs.

Classroom angle

Adding judicious to lying immediately creates a grey ethical problem: the issue is not simply lie versus truth, but judgment.

Discussion: Can a lie be morally better because it is judicious? Who decides?
Sentence model: A white lie may be defensible when it is judicious, limited, and meant to prevent unnecessary harm.
Activity: Have students define “judicious” with three conditions.
