Today's question

How can a quote become the engine of an argument instead of decoration?

Choose at least two source fragments before answering. The goal is not to sound clever first; the goal is to build a claim that has evidence behind it.

Speaking task

Give a 90-second answer to the question. Start with a concession, use one source fragment, then connect it to a real situation.

  1. State a nuanced position.
  2. Use one fragment as evidence.
  3. Give a school, family, media, or personal example.
  4. End with a clear criterion: when your position works and when it fails.

Answer shape

Although... introduce the opposite side.

What matters is... name your criterion.

This is not because..., but because... sharpen the logic.

Writing prompt

How can a quote become the engine of an argument instead of decoration? Write an essay that uses at least one source fragment from this page and one concrete example from modern life.

Revision check

  • Did you interpret the source instead of just dropping it in?
  • Did you avoid a simple yes/no answer?
  • Did each paragraph move the argument forward?
  • Did you use at least one advanced phrase from the bank?
